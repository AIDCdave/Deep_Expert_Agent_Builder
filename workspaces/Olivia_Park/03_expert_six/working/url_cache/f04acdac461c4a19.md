**The Dual Problem**

## SaaS content teams face two gaps right now. _Most are solving one and ignoring the other._

![A digital graphic consisting of a black diagonal section and a blue triangle section.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/55b249cfe4b0a4f7d765fdf1/c12e4209-bcb8-4cf5-b44c-733d0d96c4a8/Kaleigh-Blue-Triangle.png)

### Gap One: Quality

AI-assisted content is fast. Undifferentiated output is a brand liability. Most SaaS teams are now AI-assisted whether they planned for it or not. Volume is up. Quality is inconsistent. Without a human making the editorial calls, the content starts to sound like everyone else's.

- Content volume went up but engagement went down

- Brand voice is no longer consistent across channels

- No one owns the "is this good enough to publish" decision

- Writers are prompting without a strategic brief to work from


![Bold diagonal split with the top left in blue and the bottom right in black.](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/55b249cfe4b0a4f7d765fdf1/c12e4209-bcb8-4cf5-b44c-733d0d96c4a8/Kaleigh-Blue-Triangle.png)

### Gap Two: Discoverability

Ranking in Google is no longer enough. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and AI Overviews are now primary discovery channels for B2B buyers. The content strategies that worked three years ago don't get you retrieved in these systems. GEO and AEO require a different approach.

- Content doesn't surface in AI-generated answers

- Competitors are being cited in AI responses instead

- No clear strategy for generative engine optimization

- Existing SEO content isn't structured for AI retrieval


### These problems compound each other.

Generic AI-assisted content performs worse in AI search because LLMs prioritize **authoritative, structured, source-worthy content**. Closing the quality and discoverability gap requires the same strategic foundation.
