Vinay Koshy is the strongest refinement-stage addition because he directly addresses the weakest area in the first cut: credible founder-CEO ghostwriting for funded B2B technology companies. His Sproutworth materials focus specifically on funded B2B tech founders and CEOs, not generic personal branding or social media growth. The additional search results surfaced multiple long-form, recent, methodology-bearing guides: one on CEO thought-leadership ghostwriting, one on B2B CEO thought-leadership strategy, and his core Sproutworth positioning around “Pipeline Authority for Funded B2B Tech.”

His work is especially useful for Olivia because it treats executive thought leadership as a strategic operating system: extracting founder expertise, shaping it into market-facing arguments, aligning it with commercial objectives, and avoiding “brand theater” or empty engagement content. That maps tightly to Dave’s need for founder-led communication that is credible, useful, and adoption-supporting without becoming influencer-style content.

## 2. Josh Bernoff

**Primary specialization:** Clear executive and business writing; anti-bullshit editorial discipline; business-book ghostwriting and argument structure.

### Qualification rationale

Josh Bernoff remains essential because he supplies the strictest editorial judgment layer in the set. His work is highly decomposable: cut weak abstractions, remove filler, improve meaning ratio, reject unsupported claims, and force business writing to say something concrete. That is directly relevant to Olivia’s critic mode, redlining function, and anti-hype standard.

Bernoff’s books and blog provide a deep public methodology for diagnosing weak corporate prose. While he is broader than enterprise AI, his frameworks are highly transferable to founder-led AI communications because the major failure modes are the same: vague claims, inflated language, passive corporate phrasing, bloated introductions, and writing that sounds polished but does not clarify anything.

## 6. Michelle Garrett

**Primary specialization:** B2B public relations, media relations, press-release discipline, corporate announcements, and practical communications judgment.

### Qualification rationale

Michelle Garrett remains the strongest source for the press-release and announcement-writing slot. Olivia’s scope explicitly includes press releases, which require different rules than founder-authored blog posts or LinkedIn posts: third-person structure, newsworthiness, factual hierarchy, quote discipline, boilerplate, journalist relevance, and restrained corporate language.

Garrett’s work helps prevent a common failure mode: treating press releases as promotional essays. Her PR writing guidance reinforces whether an announcement is actually newsworthy, what reporters need, how claims should be supported, and how to avoid bloated launch language. That makes her essential for format discipline and brand credibility.
