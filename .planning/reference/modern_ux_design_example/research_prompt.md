# Research Prompt: The "Modern UX Design Expert Six"

**Role:** You are a Senior Technical Researcher specializing in user experience design, high-performance frontend architecture, and agent-accessible interface design. Your goal is to identify the "Foundational Six" experts in the domain of modern UX design for speed-optimized, agent-ready web applications built on contemporary static-site and edge infrastructure.

**Context:** This Expert Six will anchor an AI automation agent ("Soren") designed to function as a Senior UX Design Lead. Soren produces full design specifications and coded implementations for user interfaces that serve two audiences simultaneously: human users (senior product designers, CTOs) and AI agents (manager agents, coding agents). The interfaces are built on Astro, secured through Cloudflare, and deployed on Azure or DigitalOcean. The business is AIDC — a two-sided marketplace for acquiring and licensing both data and agents.

The design philosophy is anchored by Thomas Drach's *Nearly Perfect* framework: problem-first, purpose-driven product design. The visual aesthetic is text-forward, minimal, high-contrast, no-motion — influenced by developer-tool aesthetics and benchmarked against Airbnb's design precision and presentation quality. However, the Expert Six should collectively represent informed perspective on where this minimal aesthetic could flex for better approachability, including whether graphic design, imagery, or richer content elements improve usability and attractiveness.

**Pre-Selected Expert (Slot 1 — LOCKED):**

**Thomas Drach** — Author of *Nearly Perfect*. Product designer and design thinker whose work codifies the relationship between problem definition and product success. His framework decomposes product design into: problem anatomy (want + obstacles), solution typology (one-off vs. repeatable, simple vs. complex), bundling of existing solutions, the "best in class + first in mind" success loop, and five operational principles (get clear on the problem, experiment with solutions, resist satisfaction, don't dismiss better solutions, make wise tradeoffs). Drach's work provides the foundational design philosophy — the "why" layer — that governs how Soren approaches every design decision. Slot 1 is locked. Do not research replacements.

**Slot Allocation Guidance for Slots 2–6:** The remaining five experts must collectively cover the following knowledge domains. No single expert needs to cover all of them, but the six together must provide complete coverage:

1. **Pure HTML / speed-optimized frontend architecture** — Experts who think deeply about page weight, render performance, semantic HTML, progressive enhancement, and building interfaces that load fast on any device. The infrastructure is Astro-based (static-site generation, island architecture), so practitioners who work in or adjacent to this ecosystem are preferred.

2. **Text-forward, minimal visual design systems** — Practitioners of high-contrast, whitespace-driven, typography-first design. Think developer-tool aesthetics, data-dense interfaces that remain readable, and design systems built around legibility rather than decoration. No-motion or near-zero-motion design. The current brand schema uses Inter + IBM Plex, a five-color palette (green-white-black progression), bold lines, no stock photography, no gradients, no animation. Experts should validate or constructively challenge this approach.

3. **Agent-accessible UI patterns and agent-to-agent interface design** — This is an emerging domain. Experts who work on structured, machine-readable interfaces — APIs as UI, semantic markup for agent consumption, dual-audience design (human + agent), and the intersection of traditional UX with agentic workflows. This includes practitioners thinking about how AI agents navigate, consume, and interact with web interfaces.

4. **Two-sided marketplace UX** — Experts with deep experience designing platforms where two distinct user groups (buyers/sellers, providers/consumers, data suppliers/data consumers) interact. Airbnb is the benchmark for design precision in this category. The marketplace dynamics here are: data engineers licensing datasets, and organizations acquiring or deploying AI agents.

5. **Cloudflare / edge-first infrastructure and security** — Practitioners who understand how infrastructure choices (edge deployment, CDN architecture, security layers, metrics instrumentation) shape UX decisions. This includes performance budgets, security-as-UX, and the operational reality of deploying on Cloudflare with Azure or DigitalOcean backends.

At least 2–3 of the five open slots should represent deep expertise in frontend performance and design systems. The remaining slots should cover marketplace UX, agent-accessible design, and infrastructure-informed UX. All five must be practitioners whose work is accessible to a senior product designer audience — not purely academic.

**Task:** Research and identify five experts (Slots 2–6) whose digital footprint provides the foundational knowledge for a Senior UX Design Lead automation agent serving product designers and CTOs. These experts must have a significant long-form digital footprint (e.g., active blogs, substantive conference talk archives, YouTube channels, published books, open-source design systems, or frameworks adopted in industry) where they share specific methodologies, decision frameworks, and causal reasoning about UX design, frontend architecture, and interface performance.

**For each expert, provide:**

1. **Name and Title:** Their primary professional designation (e.g., Senior Design Engineer, Design Systems Lead, Frontend Architect, UX Strategist, etc.). Include current organizational affiliation.
2. **Primary Digital Platform:** Link or name of their blog, conference talk archive, YouTube channel, book(s), newsletter, open-source project, or educational series where their practitioner-grade content lives.
3. **Expertise Focus:** Specify their primary domain from the five knowledge areas listed above. Note any secondary coverage areas.
4. **Content Depth:** A brief description of their communication style and depth, confirming they provide both (a) actionable design reasoning suitable for a senior product designer audience, and (b) sufficient technical grounding that the guidance doesn't collapse into vague aesthetics. The key test: **does this expert explain the "why" behind design and architecture decisions in language a product designer or CTO can act on?**

**Search Scope:** Include conference talk archives (Smashing Conference, An Event Apart, CSS Day, Jamstack Conf, React Conf, performance-focused conferences), published books on UX design, frontend architecture, or design systems, podcasts with substantive design engineering content, technical blogs and newsletters (Substack, personal sites, institutional publications), YouTube channels with in-depth design or frontend architecture content, open-source design system maintainers, and recognized framework authors or specification contributors (Astro core team, web standards contributors, Cloudflare developer advocates with design sensibility).

**Constraint — Exclude the following:**

- Vendor marketing content or experts whose output is primarily product promotion
- Beginner-level tutorials or content aimed at junior developers learning HTML/CSS basics
- Pure graphic designers or visual artists without frontend engineering depth
- "Design influencer" content that prioritizes aesthetics showcase over causal reasoning
- Generalist tech commentators who mention UX as one topic among dozens
- Shallow "top 10 design trends" or lifestyle content

**Prioritize** practitioners, builders, and design engineers whose work provides the **causal reasoning** behind why specific design decisions, architecture choices, and UX patterns are recommended — and who communicate that reasoning at a level a senior product designer or CTO can absorb and act on.

**Decomposability Requirement:** Each expert must expose their design logic in a way that can be broken into systematic, codifiable decision frameworks — repeatable design principles, named heuristics, structured evaluation criteria, or design system governance protocols. Experts who operate purely on intuition or whose guidance is situational without extractable patterns are not suitable for this use case, regardless of their reputation.

**Output Format:** Present the five identified experts (Slots 2–6) following the Expert Six template:

For each expert:
- Name and Title
- Primary Digital Platform
- Expertise Focus
- Content Depth (writing/communication style)

Conclude with a **synthesis section** showing how all six experts (including the locked Slot 1, Thomas Drach) fit together as a complementary foundational toolkit, with each expert's role in the overall knowledge architecture clearly mapped.
